Dota 2 Set: Sven

Check out the Steam Page

During the course of my time at Drexel university I encountered and mastered every aspect of creating characters for games and animation. However the 5-10 week development schedules of projects at Drexel never left enough time to really dig into a single object or character and bring it to a professional level of detail. The goal of this personal project was to give myself a chance to create more meticulously crafted models. Every Texture for this armer set was meticulously hand painted in Photoshop using the same techniques employed in my digital art. Though an incredibly rewarding and fulfilling project, It illustrated for me the virtue of creating a pipeline that reaches similar quality goals but utilizes more than just my painting skills. In my following projects I have endeavoured to find a balance between this direct approaches and more procedural approaches to asset generation.

Necromancer

The creation of this project was documented in this development blog.

I developed this project simultaneously with my Master's Thesis, Dead End, over the course of a ten week term as the final submission for my graduate compositing course. A layered fully realized Photoshop painting was split and projected onto relatively simple geometry to produce the scene and character. I modeled this geometry to match the images, then rigged and animated the character. The animated scene in maya was imported and assembled in nuke for a final render. At some point I want to take another stab at this kind of composition in a game engine.
